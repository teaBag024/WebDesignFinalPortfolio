Also commonly known as the domesticated dog, you have your loyal friend who follows you around the house. Avalible in all sorts of sizes, colors and breeds. Adorable in every way, this website features many pictures and breif history of our "Man's Best Friend".


Dogs have accompanaded humans for quite the while. They started out as their closest relatives "wolves". As some of these wolves got closer to mankind, due to some mutual relationship, they started adapting to become closer to those they call their masters. It is blatantly clear that the wolves who cozied up to the humans underwent artifical selection, seeing how the now-a-day poodle and pooches defintley did not evolve from their wolf ancestors naturally. Besides the point, it is also clear that wolves who adapted to become more tolerant to humans also underwent changes in the behavioral department.

How did these wolves adapt to the humans? Well, there are a number of ways this happened, one reason being there was a mutural relationship. While humans had brand new guards to protect their adobe, these wolves recived warmth from fire and food from human scraps. There are definently many different ways that the bond bettween human and dogs occured, but suffice to say, these bondings all pretty much revolved around the same concepts. The grand take-away being that the relationship sided more towards the humans, allowing them to gain more leverage/superiority over the wolves.

Once the dogs have adapted to humans, it is only a matter of time before the humans start using the dogs in other ways then a rudimentary garbage disposal. Humans started to tame these dogs and train them to help humans hunt and better guard their places of stay. It is possible to say that humans also used these dogs militarily as well.

As humans continued to evolve, and developed agriculture, and a heirarchy system, dogs evolved too. It is redundent to say that they still guarded homes and buildings, but as human civilization started to move into a more permanent settlement, dogs started to move into their homes, and the first truly domesticated dogs started to pop-up in many civilizations. Soon, their roles eventually included babysiting, playing with humans, and helping with the family farm (sheep/cattle herding).

Dogs weren't just your average animal either, in ancient Mesopotamia, dogs were the symbol of the goddess of medicine. In Greek Mythology and Norse Mythology, dogs guarded the underworld.

It wasn't long before fashion became a thing, and many humans started looking not at their own looks, rather, to the looks of their companions. It is a no brainer that humans like the look of younger things. If you look at the common house dogs today, you could see humanity's pedophilic side showing. This is where obtaining the most cutest dogs started to to be a trend. It was around this time, artifical selection and artificial breading became popular. If we compare the three to five breeds of wolves we domestiated as cavemen(women) to the unprecidented amount of breeds of dogs we have today, we could say for sure that the fashion buisness really did a number on the dog's gene pool. If that wasn't creepy enough, we humans had he audaciy to train dogs to run through hoops and tunnels on some stage in what we now call a dog show. A once independent, proud species of Canis lupis, who hunted and could survive sub-zero degree weather, now sits on the heater and gives us googly eyes when it wants something.

That being said, many breeds of dogs are still used in their wolf-like ways. Police use dogs in search and rescue missions, mostly for their uncanny ability to smell and/or hear. Police dogs are also notorious for being used for illegal substance detetion and or weapon detection, like in airports, or other security tight places. In these cases, dogs are sent to sniff out gunpowder or specific chemicals like nitro-glycerin used in dynimite and/or firearms and explosives. Dogs are also used as service animials, helping to guide the blind, or to help people with emotional issues like PTSD and anxiety issues.

Thoes mentioned above are the icing on cakes of domesticating dogs. While it is true that the domestication of dogs gave us many advantages, not everything is sunshine and rainbows. Dogs like many other domesticated animals do have a history of spreading diseases to humans. This includes rabies, hyperkeratosis, the parvovirus, and many other influenza strains, not to mention not all dogs are coraperative with humans can cause a disaster in the house.

The domestication of dogs does not just influence the human population. Humans are also notorious for animal abuse, and pet abandonment. those whom the humans see as unfit for their current positions are put down or simply abandoned. These dogs may also experience being kicked around, or used as a punching bag. As unhumane as it seems, it is no lie, that we humans tend to be hegmonizing, and to see only what is good for ourselves then those lives around us.
