Intoduction



Addiction is an illness that lies; it is evident enough that people addicted to substances find a hard time recognizing and correcting their bad habits. Smoking is one of these addictions. It is astounding that more than 70% of adolescents have smoked at least one cigarette and, as of 2010, about 25% of the US populace smoked. Smoking is when someone inhales and exhales tobacco, toxic fumes and nicotine. Despite the countless negative effects that have been proven many times, people still choose to smoke.

To prevent more addictions and the negative effects smoking brings, it is evident and crucial that smoking is banned in public areas.

    This is by reason of :

  1. Human Health
  2. Psychological Effect of Smoking
  3. 2nd-hand Smoking
  4. Cost of Smoking (Economy)
  5. Smoking and Its Effect On the Environment



Health Hazard



Smoking is bad for human health, to help this issue, it should be banned in public areas. As noted by Ann Pietrangelo, “No matter how you smoke it, tobacco is dangerous to your health. There are no safe substances in any tobacco products,” and not only can they affect the smoker’s health, it can also affect the health of their children (Ann Pietrangelo). Issues such as lung cancer, and lung related diseases are common risks of smoking. So are heart diseases and immune system problems(Ann Pietrangelo).


Smoking being prohibited in public areas would provide people less of a window to partake in this fatal activity. When people do smoke it will be seen as disrespectful and disgusting, making it less appealing to the public. When smoking is banned, the health effects will become less common.



Psycological Effects



Smoking is bad for mental development. If smoking is prohibited in public areas, then there would be less of a role model for young minds to be influenced by. To decrease the addiction count, it is prudent to forbid smoking in public areas.


In a study by Natalia A. Goriounova and Huibert D. Mansvelder in 2012, it is noted that the adolescent brain has not completely matured. Their report further notes, “Smoking during adolescence increases the risk of developing psychiatric disorders and cognitive impairment in later life. In addition, adolescent smokers suffer from attention deficits, which aggravate with the years of smoking” (Natalia A. Goriounova, Huibert D. Mansvelder). Because smoking can do so much harm to the development of the prefrontal-cortex, it is prudent to keep adolescence from smoking. Even Goriounova and Mansvelder said so themselves, stating that adolescents “... are more influenced by smoking behavior in their social milieu. This may explain why one of five adolescents smokes regularly and up to 70% of adolescents have experimented with smoking”(Natalia A. Goriounova, Huibert D. Mansvelder).


If smoking is continued to be allowed in public areas, smokers may create the social milieu Goriounova and Mansvelder had warned of. In banning public smoking, public officials can create a better environment for young minds to grow and be influenced by. If they continue tolerance of public smoking, the reversal may be in effect. More and more adolescents will be influenced by public smokers. To prevent marring the 70% of the adolescent brains, the world's future and hope, it is critical that officials take the first steps by banning public smoking.



2nd-Hand Smoking



In addition to creating a poor public milieu as mentioned previously, it is noteworthy that smoking impacts those around them. Vulnerable people with pre-existing lung conditions may be impacted by others smoking around them, therefore it should be disallowed in public areas.


In a report in USA Today Liz Sabo had written, “...nearly 50,000 non-smokers die from secondhand smoke each year.” She continues to divuge that people exposed to secondhand smoke even have higher risks of heart diseases and cancers by up to 30% (Liz Sabo). If allowed to continue creating a society where public smoking is the norm, more and more innocent non-smokers who chose to abstain from the vulgar activity shall perish.



Economomic Outlooks



The cost in buying cigarettes and/or tobacco products weekly sums to vast amounts of money. If public smoking encourages new growths in cigarette and tobacco sales, then banning smoking in public areas can help with poverty related issues.


Mentioned in a study by Hannah Ritchie and Max Roser at OurWorldinData, the average pack of cigarettes purchased in America costs 6.07 international dollars, and the average consumption rate in the US is about 22.5 cigarettes a day (Hannah Ritchie, Max Roser).


Six dollars may seem trivial, but say there are 40 cigarettes in a pack, and the smoker smokes daily, then the smoker will go through a pack in 2 days. With the assumption that there are 30 days a month, then the average smoker would spend around $90 international dollars per month and $1080 dollars a year. This does not include the medical bill from future health problems which arise from smoking. If public officials ban public smoking, there will be less places to smoke. Therefore, not only will there be a better milieu, but a decreasing amount of places people are allowed to smoke in. This may inturn, cause people to have a lower incestive to smoke. In that case, they can save a pretty penney and actually put it towards things that will help them such as, savings, mortgage, and health care.



Enviornmental Impact



Cigarette litter is detrimental to the environment, therefore it should be interdicted in public areas.


Cigarette butts make up around 30% of all collected litter, and 75% of smokers admit to throwing their cigarette butts on the ground (5 Ways). This is detrimental to the environment due to the fact that cigarettes are composed of plastics and they leach toxic chemicals into the environment (5 Ways). The environment is already struggling with climate change and pollution, so the additional toxic waste from cigarettes is unnecessary and harmful. Prohibiting smoking in public areas can reduce the amount of cigarette litter, thus saving animals and our environment from further destruction.



Smoking needs to be banned in public areas. The ban of smoking in public areas is essential in improving the health of citizens and the environment. Smoking deteriorates the physical health of humans, it psychologically affects people, second hand smoke is harmful to bystanders, smoking costs money that could go towards longer lasting help for society, and litter from smoking is toxic to the environment. In hindsight, these reasons are just the tip of the iceberg. The lying illness, that is smoking, will only claim more victims, and destroy more lives. As the problem we created decimates us, we must take the first steps by banning public smoking. In correcting these flaws, humans step towards solving the many other problems they've created.
