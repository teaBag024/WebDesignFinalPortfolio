~Arcadia National Park~


At is best too know the rules and regulations of the park before entering. The best source is here, the National park's Arcadia page, it is also summarized below. Acadia has 27 miles of historic motor roads, 158 miles of hiking trails, and 45 miles of carriage roads. So be prepared to hike a lot. Acadia's mountains are also made of granite. As pretty as it is, granite is quite slippery when wet, so please wear apropriate shoes when hiking. Acadia also has amazing tide pools, it is a good idea to wear some type of water shoes becasue the stones can be sharp.
  • For convinence, buy then print your entrance pass online, and have it ready to display before you arrive.
  • Know before you go. Find listings of Acadia's available visitor services and facilities on the Current Conditions page.
  • No camping, campfires, or overnight parking are permitted outside of designated campgrounds. Please practice "Leave No Trace" principles.
  • Please avoid taking unnecessary risks. Hike only on designated trails.
  • All pets must always be kept on a leash no longer than six feet. Dogs are prohibited from six cliff ladder trails, public beaches (May 15-Oct 15), public buildings, and ranger-led programs.
  • Only bikes with motors that provide assistance only while the rider is pedaling, and at speeds less than 20 mph are allowed on park Carriage Roads. Please follow traffic rules.
  • Ticks Are Prevalent in Acadia and represent an increasing public health concern. Please caution yourself from them.
  • Leave Your Firewood At Home due to invasive species that may live in them. If necassary, buy local.
  • Respect Wildlife. Please don't feed, approach, or provoke any wild animals in the park.
  • Please don't contribute further to Traffic Congestion. Park legally and responsibly
