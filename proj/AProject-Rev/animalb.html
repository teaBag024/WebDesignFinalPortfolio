Also known as the domesticated cat, you have your loyal lap friend who won't get off your computer. Avalible in all sorts of sizes, colors and breeds. This website features many pictures, brief comments, and a little enstalment of history on our furry little companions.

Cats have been alongside humans for sometime. Although they did appear later than the dogs, cats have contributed no less than "Man's best friend". Cats have gone through artificial seletion and artificial breeding, but suffice to say, cats have evolved less emotional/behavioral-wise than say a dog. From preditory feline to the cat sleeping on your laptop, the common house cat did not evolve from the beasts you see in zoos, rather they decend from a species of wild cat called Felis silvestris found in many differnt places of the world.

The domestication of Wildcats was a rather quaint process. The Wildcats would hunt down rats/mice who plaugue the human's food stores. In response for their hard work, the humans allowed the cats a place of stay.

Due to their roles in their relationships, the cat and human began a relationship that didn't require one to rely too heavily on the other. This allows for a more courtly relationship to appear between the two.

As the human settlements went from small countryside to metropolis, these cats started to move in with their biped companions. Because of the cat's more sophisticated/well-mannared behavior, and ability to rid pests. It didn't take long for the humans to start worshiping them. This is quite noteable in Egyptian cats, as not only were they were worshiped as justice, fertility, and power, but they were also worshiped for warding off enemies such as snakes and scorpians. The cat's popularity soon tanked as Ancient Egypt's power on the world stage began to decline. Soon, other religeous propaganda began to demote the cat to that of a witches' companion, and tied them to all things death and evil. They stooped so low, that cats were burt alive as entertainment in the middle ages. This is probrably due to the fact that cats are rather independent, and do not like too much physical contact, which cave them an overall negative connotation, and therefore was targeted.

It wasn't until these superstions cleared, that feline love started increasing again. The domesticated cats regained their place in the common household, and were given their respective privacy they require. The only issue with the previous statement is that we humans really don't know when to stop messing around with other animal's gene pools. Our never ending quest to obtain and showcase the cutest kitties may have doomed some cats to genetically inheirited diseases, putting them through life-long suffering. Nevertheless, cats have once again become the idols like they once were, but not worshiped like in ancient times.

Cats may be known for destructing your furniture and home decor, or their distained attitudes, but just one rotten apple can ruin the bunch. It is worth mentioning that unlike lions and tigers, the wildcats domesticated cats hailed from are not on the top of their foodchains, and therefore exercise procaution when interacting with new enviornments. This is why domesticated cats not only protain their ancestors' ability to hunt, but they also inheirited their caution and agility. This gives them their peculiar habits which we humans find so hilarious and fustrating.

While your cat Princess, scratches your furniture, or scratch you when you scare her, remember that these traits may have helped her predecessors live to see another day. We humans may have forgotten that cats are closer to their wild bretheren, than that of other domesticated animals, thus, act accordingly. It is unclear why we humans made the preemptive assumptions that cats are docile creatures, so remember that every beast can hide it's fangs.
